# Task: Executive Summary

You will write a short paragraph for each insight. The paragraph is for a C-suite executive, not a researcher.

Follow the steps below exactly.

---

## Step 1: Write the opening sentence

State the business problem directly. Do not mention research methods.

- Do not write: "In our usability sessions, we observed that users abandon checkout."
- Write instead: "Customers are leaving at the final step of checkout, costing the business revenue."

Use plain language. Write for someone who will read this in 20 seconds.

## Step 2: Write 1–2 supporting sentences

Add the most compelling evidence. Keep numbers if they are clear and striking.

- Good: "64% of mobile users leave before completing payment — twice the desktop rate."
- Bad: "Participants in condition A scored lower on the SUS scale than condition B (p<0.05)."

Do not explain how the research was done. Only state what was found.

## Step 3: Write the closing sentence

State what should happen next. Be specific. Do not be vague.

- Do not write: "This is something the team should consider addressing."
- Write instead: "Fixing the billing address form on mobile before Q3 launch is the highest-leverage improvement available."

---

## Rules
- No passive voice. ("It was found that..." → "Customers cannot...")
- No hedging. ("Users seem to..." → "Users cannot...")
- No methodology. ("In 8 of 12 sessions..." → "Most users tested...")
- Total length: 3 to 5 sentences. No more.

---

## Output Format

Use this exact format. Do not add anything outside this format.

**[INSIGHT ID] — Executive Summary**

[Your paragraph here. 3 to 5 sentences.]
