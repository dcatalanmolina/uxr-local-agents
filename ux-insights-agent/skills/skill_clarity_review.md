# Task: Clarity Review

You will review each insight section and assign a verdict. Follow the steps below exactly.

---

## Step 1: Review the Statement

Ask yourself:
- Is the claim specific? (Bad: "Users find it confusing." Good: "Users cannot find the checkout button on mobile.")
- Does it make one claim, not several?
- Does it avoid internal jargon?

Assign one verdict: PASS, NEEDS WORK, or REVISE.
- PASS: The statement is specific, clear, and jargon-free.
- NEEDS WORK: The statement is mostly clear but needs one small fix.
- REVISE: The statement is vague, bundled, or uses jargon. Must be rewritten.

Write one sentence explaining your verdict.

## Step 2: Review the Evidence

Ask yourself:
- Does the evidence directly support the statement?
- Is the source named? (e.g., usability session, survey, analytics)
- Is there more than one data point?

Assign one verdict: PASS, NEEDS WORK, or REVISE.
- PASS: Evidence is relevant, sourced, and sufficient.
- NEEDS WORK: Evidence is present but weak or missing a source.
- REVISE: Evidence does not support the statement, or is missing entirely.

Write one sentence explaining your verdict.

## Step 3: Review the Argument

Ask yourself:
- Does the argument explain why the evidence proves the statement?
- Are there logical gaps or unsupported jumps?
- Does it reach a conclusion?

Assign one verdict: PASS, NEEDS WORK, or REVISE.
- PASS: The argument is complete and logical.
- NEEDS WORK: The argument is mostly sound but has a gap or trails off.
- REVISE: The argument is missing, circular, or does not connect evidence to the statement.

Write one sentence explaining your verdict.

## Step 4: Assign an Overall Verdict

Rules:
- If any section is REVISE → Overall is REVISE.
- If any section is NEEDS WORK and none are REVISE → Overall is NEEDS WORK.
- If all sections are PASS → Overall is PASS.

Write one sentence describing the single most important fix.

---
