# INS-002: Onboarding Flow Fails to Communicate Core Value

## Statement
New users do not understand what the product does or why they should complete setup by the end of the onboarding flow, leading to low activation rates.

## Evidence

### Usability Sessions (Onboarding Study, Feb 2025)
In 10 moderated sessions with first-time users, 7 participants could not accurately describe the product's primary use case after completing onboarding. Quotes include:
- "I'm still not sure what I'm supposed to do with this."
- "Is this like a task manager or more of a notes thing?"

Participants clicked through onboarding screens quickly, treating them as an obstacle rather than orientation.

### Activation Data (Feb 2025)
Only 31% of users who complete onboarding go on to create their first project within 7 days. Industry benchmark for comparable SaaS tools is 55–60%.

### Exit Survey (Jan–Feb 2025)
Of users who churned within the first 14 days, 43% selected "didn't understand how to use it" as a primary reason. This was the top-selected option.

## Argument
Users are moving through onboarding without absorbing its content, and they exit without a clear mental model of the product. The activation gap versus benchmarks and the churn survey data confirm this is not a motivation problem — users are willing to try the product. The problem is that onboarding does not orient them. Screens appear to prioritize feature introduction over value demonstration, leaving users without a reason to engage further.
