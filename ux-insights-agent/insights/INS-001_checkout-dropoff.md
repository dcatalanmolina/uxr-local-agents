# INS-001: Checkout Drop-off at Payment Step

## Statement
Users abandon the checkout flow at a significantly higher rate on the payment entry screen than at any other step, primarily due to confusion around the billing address form.

## Evidence

### Usability Sessions (Round 3, March 2025)
In 8 of 12 moderated usability sessions, participants paused or expressed verbal frustration at the billing address step. Common issues included:
- The "same as shipping address" checkbox was not visible without scrolling on mobile
- The form required a full street address even when autofill had already populated the field
- Two participants abandoned the task entirely at this step, stating the form "felt broken"

### Analytics (Q1 2025)
Funnel analysis shows a 64% drop-off rate at the payment step on mobile, compared to 22% on desktop. This is the largest single drop-off point in the entire checkout flow. The mobile drop-off rate has increased from 48% in Q3 2024.

### Support Tickets (Jan–Mar 2025)
17 support tickets in Q1 referenced checkout issues. 11 of those specifically mentioned the payment or billing address screen.

## Argument
The convergence of qualitative frustration in sessions, a widening gap between mobile and desktop drop-off rates, and a high volume of related support tickets points to a specific, addressable UX failure at the billing address step — not a general reluctance to complete checkout. The mobile-specific severity suggests the form layout is not responsive at key breakpoints. This is not a trust or pricing issue; it is a form usability issue.
